## §4 — Key Technical Methods

### Governing equations (TOUGH2 lineage — the spine of the review)
All written in **integral form** over an arbitrary subdomain Vₙ (this is *why* IFDM is the natural discretization):

- **Master balance** (per component κ = 1…NK mass components + κ = NK+1 for heat):
  d/dt ∫ Mᵏ dV = ∫ Fᵏ·n dΓ + ∫ qᵏ dV
- **Mass accumulation:** Mᵏ = φ Σ_β S_β ρ_β Xᵏ_β  (couples water to BOTH liquid & vapour phases — the boiling/two-phase core)
- **Energy accumulation:** M^(NK+1) = (1−φ) ρ_R C_R T + φ Σ_β S_β ρ_β u_β  (rock-energy storage dominates)
- **Multiphase Darcy flux:** F_β = −k (k_{r,β} ρ_β / μ_β)(∇P_β − ρ_β g)
- **Heat flux (conduction + convection):** F^(NK+1) = −λ∇T + Σ_β h_β F_β  (latent heat of steam rides in h_β → vapour-dominated heat transport)
- **Diffusive flux (optional):** Jᵏ_β = −φ τ_β ρ_β dᵏ_β ∇Xᵏ_β  (minor in high-flow reservoirs)

### Numerical methods
- **Integral Finite Difference Method (IFDM)** (Narasimhan & Witherspoon 1976): discretizes the integral form directly,
  no global coordinate system → arbitrary 1/2/3-D irregular grids; equivalent to conventional FD on regular grids
  ("no particular price" for the flexibility). A block is characterized only by volume Vₙ, interface areas A_nm, nodal
  distances Dₙ+Dₘ, gravity projection g_nm.
- **Weighting:** absolute permeability **harmonically** weighted; relative permeability (mobility) **100% single-point
  upstream** weighted — required (with full implicitness) for stability through phase appearance/disappearance.
- **Time:** fully implicit, first-order backward; adaptive time-step control.
- **Newton-Raphson** on the residual form Rᵏₙ(x^(t+1)) = 0, Jacobian by numerical perturbation; primary-variable
  switching by phase state (single-phase: P,T ; two-phase: P_gas, S_gas).
- **EOS modules** (modular): **EOS1** (pure water/steam — standard geothermal), EOS2 (water+CO₂), EOS3 (water+air),
  **EWASG** (water+NaCl+NCG, with salt precip/perm change). Water props: IFC-1967 steam tables.
- **Fractured media:** double-porosity (Warren & Root 1963) / **MINC** (Pruess & Narasimhan 1985) as IFDM mesh
  preprocessing (MESHMAKER).

### Inverse modelling — iTOUGH2 (Finsterle, LBNL)
Automatic calibration replacing manual history matching. Minimizes weighted-least-squares objective
**S = rᵀ C_zz⁻¹ r = Σ_i (z*_i − z_i)² / σ_i²** (max-likelihood under independent-normal residuals; robust L1/Huber
options). Levenberg-Marquardt (Gauss-Newton ↔ steepest descent); sensitivities by finite-difference perturbation
(repeated forward TOUGH2 runs). Delivers parameter covariance C_pp (uncertainties, correlations), residual diagnostics,
and FOSM/Monte-Carlo uncertainty propagation onto predictions. Canonical geothermal cite: **Finsterle & Pruess (1995),
17th NZ Geothermal Workshop, 193–198.**

### The standard modelling workflow (the review's methodological core)
Conceptual model → grid design + property/EOS assignment → **natural-state** calibration (vs downhole T/P) →
**production history match** (vs pressure/enthalpy/flow) → **future-performance prediction** (scenarios, reinjection,
sustainability) → iterate as monitoring data arrive. *Reservoir simulation is a continuously-updated process, not a
one-off.*
