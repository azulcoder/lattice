## §3 — Paper Structure (read directly from the Stanford PDF — HIGH confidence)

Outline (faithful to the PDF): **Abstract · Introduction · Exploration Approaches** (anomaly hunting / anomaly stacking
/ conceptual models) **· Geothermal Conceptual Models** (interpreting isotherms on cross-sections; upflow & outflow;
realistic isotherm patterns; permeable / impermeable / juxtaposed zones; cooler water entering an upflow) **· Building a
Conceptual Model** (well data; geochemistry; geophysics) **· Blind Geothermal Systems** (exploration data; exploration
scenario) **· Conclusions · References.**

**Thesis (one line):** the single most important element for targeting a geothermal well or assessing resource capacity
at the exploration stage is a **resource conceptual model** consistent with *all* data, whose core is a **predicted
natural-state isotherm pattern viewed in cross-section** — because single-dataset "anomaly hunting" and "anomaly
stacking" mislead (low-resistivity / magnetic lows occur over barren valleys as well as reservoirs), while a well that
tests an *interpreted isotherm* is directly testable and supports a "learning" workflow.

**Conceptual-model components the paper assembles:** heat source / deep fracture-hosted upflow; vertical upflow zone;
lateral outflow zone (with the diagnostic temperature reversal); low-permeability smectite clay cap; hydrothermal
alteration zones (smectite→illite transition as a temperature/resistivity calibration); reservoir (two archetypes,
below); permeable vs impermeable zones read from isotherm spacing; recharge / cooler-water inflow; the natural-state
isotherm distribution (the most important element); structural controls; water table & fluid types.

**Two generic reservoir archetypes:** (1) ~250–300 °C **volcanic, fracture/intrusion-dominated** upflow (the
vapour-dominated / dry-steam family — Darajat, The Geysers, Larderello, Kamojang); (2) ~150–200 °C **Basin-and-Range
fault-fed liquid outflow**. Plus a synthetic **blind-prospect** scenario (sediments over Paleozoic schist) illustrating
the **graphitic-schist resistivity-low pitfall**.

## §4 — Key Methods

1. **MT/AMT resistivity clay-cap interpretation.** MT images subsurface resistivity in cross-section; the diagnostic
   target is the conductive (<~10 ohm-m, <5 ohm-m core) **smectite clay cap** in the argillic halo above/around the
   upflow. Resistivity tracks *alteration mineralogy* more than present temperature, so the **base of the cap** maps the
   smectite→illite/chlorite transition (~180–220 °C). The upflow sits beneath the structural high / thinnest cap;
   outflow plumes read as the cap dipping laterally. **Cumming's central caution: low resistivity is non-unique** (clay
   valley fill, saline groundwater, graphitic schist) — integrate, never use as a stand-alone anomaly.
2. **Gas + cation geothermometry.** Fumarole/steam gas geothermometers constrain vapour-zone/upflow temperatures;
   Na-K / Na-K-Ca / silica geothermometers on neutral-chloride spring waters constrain liquid-reservoir temperatures.
   Used as fixed points the isotherms must honour; cross-check which springs are true upflow/outflow vs steam-heated/
   mixed (which read misleadingly low).
3. **Boiling-point-for-depth (BPD) curve.** For a *liquid* boiling upflow, max temperature at depth is fixed by the
   hydrostatic BPD relation; combined with a geothermometer temperature it converts one number into a depth–temperature
   isotherm anchor. **⟦TODO-Az: BPD is LIQUID-specific; for dry-steam Darajat the deep zone is near-isothermal
   vapour-static, not on the BPD curve — see §5 example 2 and §9.⟧**
4. **Reading permeability from isotherm spacing** (the interpretive heart): widely spaced isotherms = permeable,
   convection-dominated upflow/outflow; closely spaced = low-permeability conductive lid (the clay cap).
5. **Integration of geology / structure / hydrology / surface alteration** — the model must satisfy *all* datasets
   simultaneously; this is what separates conceptual-model targeting from anomaly hunting/stacking.
6. **Temperature-gradient & slim holes** — the only direct subsurface temperature before full-diameter drilling; a
   reversal confirms an outflow plume, a near-isothermal profile a permeable convective target. The validation/iteration
   step that updates the cross-section and reduces well-targeting risk.

**Capacity estimation (Cumming's companion method):** resource **capacity = AREA (km²) × POWER DENSITY (MWe/km²)**, two
independent **lognormals** → the product is lognormal, sampled by **Monte Carlo** → report P90/P50/P10 MWe, then apply a
separate **probability-of-exploration-success (POSₑₓₚₗ)** for a *risked* capacity to rank prospects. Power density is
**system-dependent** — calibrate against analog producing fields of the same type.
