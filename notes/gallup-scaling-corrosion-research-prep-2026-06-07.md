## 2. Scaling — backbone (standard engineering; verified)
- The dissolved load that geothermometry *reads* (Giggenbach module) becomes a
  production *liability* when the fluid cools and flashes on the way up.
- **Silica scaling** (the dominant problem in high-T liquid fields): amorphous silica
  deposits once the fluid cools below silica saturation, and **flashing concentrates the
  remaining brine** (boiling off steam raises dissolved-solids concentration), pushing
  silica supersaturated. Silica then **polymerises** (kinetically controlled) and
  deposits in surface pipes, separators, and especially **injection wells**. Controls:
  keep the fluid **above silica saturation temperature** (don't over-cool), manage
  **residence/retention time** so polymerised silica is removed before deposition,
  **acidify / pH-modify** (Gallup's pH-mod) to slow polymerisation, dose **inhibitors**,
  or **dilute**.
- **Calcite (CaCO₃) scaling**: driven by **CO₂ loss at the flash point** — when the
  fluid first boils, CO₂ partitions into the steam, the liquid pH rises, and calcite
  becomes supersaturated and deposits right **at/just above the flash horizon in the
  wellbore** (a classic geothermal wellbore-plugging problem). Controls: **downhole
  scale-inhibitor injection** (capillary string below the flash point), or operate so the
  flash is at surface.

## 3. Corrosion & gas — backbone (standard engineering; verified)
- **Corrosion** agents: **H₂S, CO₂, chloride, low pH, and O₂ ingress** (especially in
  cooled condensate and injection lines). Failure modes: sulfide stress cracking,
  pitting, general wall loss. Controls: **material selection** (corrosion-resistant
  alloys where needed), **inhibitors**, and **excluding oxygen**.
- **Non-condensable gas (NCG) / H₂S management**: CO₂ and H₂S come out with the steam,
  loading the condenser/gas-extraction system and requiring **H₂S abatement** (e.g.
  to elemental sulfur). Mercury management can also matter.
- **Monitoring**: silica-polymerisation kinetics, total suspended solids (TSS),
  corrosion coupons.
