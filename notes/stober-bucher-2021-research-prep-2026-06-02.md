## §4 — Key Concepts (teachable, with formulas where clean)

1. **Geothermal gradient & terrestrial heat flow** — Fourier's law `q = -k dT/dz`; continental gradient ~25-30 °C/km;
   mean continental heat flow ~60-65 mW/m² (oceanic ~100). The foundational driver.
2. **Conductive vs convective transport** — Péclet number `Pe = ρ_f c_f v L / λ` (Pe≫1 → flowing-water-dominated
   hydrothermal convection; Pe≪1 → conductive geotherm); free convection onset at a critical Rayleigh number (~40 for a
   layer heated from below). Separates conductive "hot dry rock" (EGS targets) from naturally convecting hydrothermal.
3. **Darcy flow & transmissivity** — `q = -(k/μ)∇P`; `K = kρg/μ`; transmissivity `T = K·b` sets well-test deliverability.
4. **Hydrothermal vs petrothermal (EGS)** — hydrothermal = hot mobile fluid in permeable rock (produce the fluid);
   petrothermal/HDR = heat in low-permeability rock, accessed by engineering fracture permeability (stimulation).
5. **Enthalpy / temperature classes** — low (<~100 °C, direct/GSHP), medium (~100-150), high (>~150-180 °C, power);
   enthalpy `h = h_f + x·h_fg` is the rigorous classifier (captures steam fraction). **⟦TODO-Az: exact high/low
   threshold not standardized (150/190/200 °C); don't attribute a specific cutoff to the book without page check.⟧**
6. **Ground-source heat pump vs deep geothermal** — shallow = heat-pump-upgraded low-grade heat, `COP = Q_heat/W`
   (Carnot ceiling `T_hot/(T_hot−T_cold)`); deep = direct use of naturally hot fluid.
7. **Water-rock interaction** — scaling (calcite, silica, sulfates) + corrosion (Cl, CO₂/H₂S, low pH); solute
   geothermometers invert dissolved species to deep temperature, e.g. quartz `T = 1309/(5.19 − log₁₀[SiO₂]) − 273.15`.
