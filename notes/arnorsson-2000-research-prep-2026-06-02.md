## §3 — Structure & Scope (15 chapters)

A multidisciplinary instructional handbook: from geochemical strategy, chemical equilibria, and the **reactive vs
conservative** component distinction, through a dedicated **isotopes** treatment (stable δD/δ¹⁸O, tritium, carbon) and
the origin of chemical/isotopic species, into detailed **field sampling protocols** (springs, fumaroles, **wet-steam
(two-phase) wells**, separated water and steam, gases/condensate) and **analytical accuracy / charge-balance / QA**,
then the interpretive core: **solute and gas geothermometry**, **mixing models** in upflow zones, **reconstruction of
deep aquifer fluid composition from wet-steam well data + enthalpy**, **H/O isotopic fractionation during boiling**,
mineral-saturation calculations, **aquifer steam-fraction** estimation, and **reservoir-response monitoring** during
production. It deliberately builds on Henley-Truesdell-Barton (1984), extending it with rigorous isotope techniques and
standardized procedures, and is the authoritative modern reference for geothermal geochemical/isotope investigation.

## §4 — Key Concepts (with formulas; isotopes are the distinctive layer)

1. **δ-notation on the VSMOW scale.** `δ = (R_sample/R_VSMOW − 1)·1000 ‰`, with `R = ²H/¹H` (δD) or `¹⁸O/¹⁶O` (δ¹⁸O);
   meteoric waters are negative (lighter than ocean water). The foundational stable-isotope measurement.
2. **Global Meteoric Water Line (Craig 1961).** `δD = 8·δ¹⁸O + 10 ‰` — worldwide precipitation plots on this line
   (slope 8 from equilibrium condensation fractionation; +10 ‰ deuterium excess). The reference baseline.
3. **The oxygen-18 shift (water-rock exchange).** Geothermal reservoir waters plot to the RIGHT of the GMWL: at high T
   the water exchanges ¹⁸O with ¹⁸O-rich rock, enriching δ¹⁸O while **δD stays nearly constant** (rock has little H).
   `Δ¹⁸O = δ¹⁸O_geothermal − δ¹⁸O_local-meteoric`, a near-horizontal shift; magnitude scales with T, rock δ¹⁸O, and
   water/rock ratio.
4. **Tracing recharge via conserved δD.** Because δD survives the oxygen shift, projecting a geothermal water
   horizontally back to the (Local) Meteoric Water Line gives the recharge δD; with the **altitude effect**
   (~−1.5 to −4 ‰ δD per 100 m) this locates the recharge area/elevation. Heavier-δD deviations flag seawater,
   evaporated, or magmatic/andesitic water.
5. **Tritium (³H).** Radioactive H (half-life **12.32 yr**), in Tritium Units; entered groundwater via 1950s-60s
   bomb testing. Detectable ³H → a "young" (post-~1952) recharge or shallow-mixing component; tritium-dead → old,
   well-isolated deep water. A residence-time / mixing tracer.
6. **Sampling & reconstruction.** The practical heart: sample two-phase (wet-steam) wells correctly, and **reconstruct
   the deep aquifer fluid** from the separated water + steam compositions and the measured enthalpy; correct everything
   for boiling/degassing before geothermometry (the QA discipline). Solute + gas geothermometry then follow (extending
   Henley-Truesdell-Barton).
